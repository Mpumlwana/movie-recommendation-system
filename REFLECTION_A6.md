# Reflection For Assignment 6

## Challenges Faced

One of the main challenges in this assignment was prioritizing user stories. It was difficult to determine which features were most important for the MVP while balancing system functionality and user needs.

Another challenge was estimating effort using story points. Since the system is not yet implemented, estimating complexity required assumptions and careful judgment.

Breaking down user stories into smaller tasks was also challenging, as it required thinking from a developer’s perspective and ensuring tasks were clear and manageable.

Another significant challenge was internal resistance and decision-making. Since I was acting as both the product owner and developer, it was sometimes difficult to make objective decisions about priorities. I had to balance my own preferences with what would realistically deliver the most value in the MVP.

## Lessons Learned

This assignment helped me understand how Agile methodology structures development into manageable and iterative steps. User stories make requirements more user-focused and easier to implement.

I also learned how prioritization frameworks like MoSCoW help in focusing on delivering the most important features first.

Additionally, sprint planning demonstrated how to organize work efficiently and align development tasks with project goals.

## Conclusion

Overall, this assignment strengthened my understanding of Agile planning and execution. It demonstrated how to transform system requirements into actionable development steps and plan work effectively for incremental delivery.
